Do's & Don'ts in Singapore

тЬЕ DoтАЩs
  • Do carry cash: While digital payments are common, some hawker centers and small shops prefer cash, especially Singapore dollars (SGD).
  • Do carry identification: Keep your passport or ID handy, as police may conduct random checks.
  • Be punctual for meetings and appointments.
  • Respect local customs and multicultural diversity.
  • Use public transport (MRT, buses) тАФ clean, efficient, and safe.
  • Stand on the left side of escalators; let others pass on the right.
  • Dispose of trash properly тАФ Singapore is known for its cleanliness.
тЭМ DonтАЩts
  • Don't feed wild animals: Feeding animals like monkeys can result in significant fines.┬а
  • DonтАЩt litter, spit, or smoke in prohibited areas.
  • DonтАЩt eat or drink on public transport тАФ itтАЩs against the law.
  • DonтАЩt engage in public displays of affection in conservative areas.
  • DonтАЩt bring chewing gum into Singapore illegally.
  • DonтАЩt criticize religion or race тАФ Singapore enforces harmony strictly.
  • DonтАЩt take photos without consent: Respect privacy, especially in private spaces or when photographing locals or religious sites.

ЁЯз│ Travel Tips for Visitors

1. Getting Around ЁЯЪЖ

The MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) and public buses cover almost all parts of Singapore. Buy an **EZ-Link card** or use your contactless bank card for convenience.

2. Weather ЁЯМжя╕П

Singapore is hot and humid year-round (around 30┬░C). Carry water, wear light clothes, and bring an umbrella for sudden rain showers.

3. SIM Cards & Wi-Fi ЁЯУ▒

Buy a **prepaid SIM card** from Singtel, StarHub, or M1 at the airport. Free public Wi-Fi is available at malls and MRT stations via Wireless@SG.

4. Money & Payments ЁЯТ│

Currency: Singapore Dollar (SGD). Cards are accepted almost everywhere. For small shops or hawker centres, carry some cash.

5. Health & Safety ЁЯЪС

Tap water is safe to drink. Singapore is one of the worldтАЩs safest countries тАФ but always watch your belongings in crowded areas.

6. Best Time to Visit ЁЯУЕ

Singapore can be visited year-round. Major festivals include **Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb)**, **Deepavali (Oct/Nov)**, and **National Day (Aug 9)**.
